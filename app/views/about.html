Frequently Asked Questions

What is Paradrop?

Paradrop is a research project out of the WiNGS Laboratory in the Computer Sciences department at The University of Wisconsin - Madison.

It is a framework that allows third party developers access to computational resources on access points (APs or gateways) within the home. Its goal is to bring networking intelligence in the home through the replacement of commodity WiFi AP hardware.

This will provide many unique capabilities to all entities who come in contact with networking-related technology including:

  • end-users
  • developers
  • ISPs
  • content distributors

Where can I get a Paradrop Router?

We are currently perfecting out hardware platform, and so at this time we do not have a commercial product available. We have already released some prototypes and are moving forward with a main build idea. Please join our interest list for more information.

Why name it Paradrop?

By definition, a paradrop is a deployment of supplies to a mobile or not well-travelled location.

This is exactly what Paradrop does -- it provides supplies (computational resources) to developers in a location that is traditionally difficult to access, the home.

Why might I want computational resources in my home?

Paradrop enables the decoupling of the network control plane for many categories of applications in a way that isn't possible by other means. What this allows is a network-based approach to implement applications that are agnostic to the end points of a service.
